NO HOME IN THE WASTE
Chapter 2 — The Cabin

Day 14 – The Foot of the Mountains

The air smells different here—pine and damp soil instead of ash. The silence presses on the chest like the world’s holding its breath.

She walks ahead most of the time. Never looks back. I think she likes not having to see me.

At night, I catch her staring into the fire, her eyes reflecting the light but not feeling it. I ask if she’s okay. She says, “I’m fine.” She’s not.

Day 15 – The Cabin

We found it today—a cabin by a creek, half-eaten by moss. The chimney still stood. The door was open.

It smelled wrong. Not just rot—something soaked into the wood. I told her to wait. She didn’t.

She gripped the machete and went in first. The door slammed against the wall. A sound came from inside—wet, hungry, too fast.

The first feral lunged from the dark and she met it head-on, blade flashing. The sound of it hitting the floor was like meat dropped on stone. Another came from the hall, another from under the stairs.

She didn’t hesitate. She charged straight into them, hacking and screaming, every swing sharper, angrier, faster. It wasn’t fighting—it was exorcism.

When the last one fell, she didn’t stop. She kept swinging. Again. And again. Until her arms gave out and her breath came in gasps.

I grabbed her wrist to stop her. She turned on me like she didn’t know who I was—eyes wild, teeth bared. I had to hold her, drag her out onto the porch. She fought me until her knees hit the dirt, and then she broke.

We burned the bodies before dark. The smoke twisted up into the gray sky and vanished. She didn’t look away once.

When it was done, the forest was still. Even the wind wouldn’t come near.

We slept inside that night. The cabin’s ours now. It doesn’t feel like home.

Day 18 – Cleaning

She hasn’t stopped working. Scrubbing blood off floorboards, hammering shutters, fixing cracks that don’t matter. Her hands are torn open.

I told her to rest. She said, “If I stop, I’ll think.” At night she keeps the machete beside her bed. Sometimes I wake to the sound of her whispering his name.
