HF43 — Chapter 7: The Founder (Tightened)

The fires over Harper’s Ferry had dimmed, but the air still hummed with static. HF43’s main gate was sealed, reinforced by scavenged plating from the old armory. Inside, the survivors worked like ghosts under the green light of terminals, each screen flickering lines of forgotten code.

They said the Founder had been part of the first experiment — the one that gave HF43 its name. Records were lost, scrubbed from the Vault-Tec archives, but a rumor persisted: the Founder never left. Somewhere in the tunnels beneath the reactor, a voice still answered to the code HF43.

When the last terminal reactivated, the message came through in bursts. Static first, then a single repeating fragment: “System integrity compromised. HF43 integrity—” the voice cut, looped, then flared again. The HF43 marker blinked once, twice, three times before fading out.

Most thought it was noise. But a few of us saw the pattern. Three pulses, three pauses — an invitation.
