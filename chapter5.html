NO HOME IN THE WASTE
Chapter 5 — The Change

Day 54 – The Raid

The fever came fast, hard, and stayed. Cold packs, herbal tea—nothing worked. We needed real medicine.

After three sleepless nights, I packed the knife and the rucksack. The old pharmacy was twenty miles east.

Three scavengers had made it home. I moved quiet, knife in hand. When it was done, I was shaking—not from fear, but from how easy it had been.

Then I heard a song. A broken lullaby on a loop. I followed it to a back room and found a little girl, clutching a cracked radio. She looked up and said, “Is my mom with you?” I told her no. She asked, “Can I come?” I said yes.

Day 55 – Back at the Cabin

When I carried her inside, my wife stirred. She smiled weakly and whispered, “He’s home.” I didn’t correct her.

The girl’s laughter filled the place again. For a while, it felt almost right. We taught her to boil water, to draw, to hope.

But the fever only grew worse. Her skin cracked, her eyes clouded. She stopped speaking in full sentences. Just whispers, low and strange.

Day 58 – The River

I went fishing this morning. She was asleep when I left. The girl was outside humming with her little radio.

When I came back, the door was open.

Blood on the walls. The girl on the floor. Her radio skipping on a single note.

My wife stood over her, trembling, glowing faint green. She turned to me, smiling wrong. “Hungry,” she said.

I raised the gun. “I’m sorry.” Then I pulled the trigger.

Day 59 – Ashes

I buried them together beside the memorial. When the graves were covered, I poured the last of the whisky over the dirt. Then I burned the cabin.

The flames climbed quick—wood dry, air thick with rot and smoke. I stood there until the roof caved, until the world stopped glowing.

There’s nothing left here but ghosts.

I packed the journal, the knife, and started walking east. The sky is gray again. The air hums with that same broken note.

There is no home in the waste.
