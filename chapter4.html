NO HOME IN THE WASTE
Chapter 4 — The Green Sky

Day 46 – Morning

The frost melted off the roof today. Sunlight slipped through the cracks like gold dust. For the first time in months, she smiled—not forced, not hollow. A real one.

The wildflowers are blooming. She calls them resurrection blooms. Says they only grow after the earth’s been burned.

We fixed the roof, patched the fence, and cleared land near the creek. She says it’s starting to feel like home again. I think she might be right.

Day 46 – Afternoon

She wanted a day off. “Just us,” she said. “A day that isn’t about surviving.”

We packed two cans of peaches, some bread, a bit of smoked cheese, and a couple of beers that hadn’t gone flat yet.

We hiked up the hill behind the cabin to a spot overlooking the valley. From there, everything looked whole again. For a few hours, it didn’t feel like the end of the world.

“Do you ever think,” she asked, “that maybe everything broken still wants to heal?” I told her maybe that’s what we were doing—healing, piece by piece. She smiled. “Then maybe we’re not broken anymore.”

Day 46 – Evening

She was right—it was our anniversary. I went back to the cabin to fetch something special: a bottle of Pappy Van Winkle 23 I’d found months ago scavenging alone. I’d hidden it under a floorboard.

As I brushed off the dust, I heard it—deep, low, distant. Not thunder. It sounded alive.

I stepped outside just as it rose over the ridge—a massive, winged creature. Its hide glowed faint green, light leaking from cracks in its skin. The air trembled around it.

I thought it was a plane. Then the sky began to fall.

A mist—green and shimmering—poured from its body, drifting like ash. She was still on the hill. Laughing.

I screamed her name. She turned. The dust reached her before my voice did.
